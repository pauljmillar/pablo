Our TechEd Philosophy

Sculpting well-rounded, curious, responsible tech minds
Consumers and Producers Teaching students to be both
Our goals for our TechEd curriculum vary with the child's age, but our philosophy is consistent across ages: teaching children how to be responsible, informed consumers of technology, while developing their understanding and skills necessary to produce new technology.
For our youngest children, consuming means being comfortable with a keyboard, mouse, tablet, touchpad.
For children 8-10, consuming means understanding internet safety and privacy and a responsible introduction to social media. They'll produce programs, presentations, and websites.
For middle schoolers, consuming includes responsible internet research and safely using social media. They'll produce websites, robots, and podcasts. Browse 3rd Grade
Leading Technology Platforms Mastery of leading platforms
Our curriculum blends leading, established platforms and strategies that have been proven and tested by millions of students. This includes computer science and thinking exercises from Code.org, block programming from Scratch; document and presentation creation and colaboration using Google Docs; an introduction to databases by Codecademy; website creation by Mozilla; how the internet works with lessons on Khan Academy; software to hardware cooperation with lessons from Kano; and many many others.

Figuring Out, Not Memorizing Learning by doing
Our lessons expose children to A LOT. The goal is to prepare them to be responsible users of technology, and curious creators with technology - not to learn new vocabulary. We encourage the children to learn by doing, by trying, and by thinking, rather than by reading a lesson. Of course, there are concepts to introduce, but for the most part, our lessons guide students through "doing" something. Browse 6th Grade
